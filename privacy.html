Dinner Wheel — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 15, 2026

Dinner Wheel ("the app") helps you decide what to eat by spinning a wheel of local takeout / fast‑food spots and trending cook‑at‑home recipes. We built it to be simple and privacy‑respecting. We do not have user accounts, we do not run ads, and we do not sell your data.

TL;DR
  • The app has no backend server of its own — it runs on your device.
  • It uses your location only to find restaurants near you, and only when you ask it to.
  • We don't store your location. It is sent to a third‑party map service to look up nearby places, then discarded.
  • No advertising, no analytics/tracking, no data sales.

Information the app uses

  • Approximate / precise location — Only if you tap to find nearby food (or type a city/ZIP). Your device coordinates (or the place you type) are used to search for restaurants within your chosen distance. We do not keep a copy and we do not link it to your identity.
  • Your in‑app choices — Dietary/allergy filters, distance range, and a daily cache of trending recipes are stored locally on your device (browser localStorage). They never leave your device.

Third‑party services we call

To provide its features, the app makes requests to these services. Your location (when used) is shared only with the mapping services below, and only to fetch nearby places:

  • OpenStreetMap Overpass API — finds restaurants around your location. See the OSM Foundation privacy policy.
  • OpenStreetMap Nominatim — converts a city/ZIP you type into map coordinates. Same OSM privacy policy as above.
  • TheMealDB — supplies trending cook‑at‑home recipes. No location or personal data is sent. See themealdb.com.
  • Google Maps — only if you tap "Open in Google Maps" on a result, which opens Google Maps with the restaurant name. Governed by Google's privacy policy.

What we do NOT do

  • No user accounts or sign‑in.
  • No advertising or ad networks.
  • No analytics, tracking, or behavioral profiling.
  • No selling or sharing of personal data with data brokers.
  • No server‑side storage of your location or activity.

Permissions

  • Location — used only to find food near you; you can deny it and type a city/ZIP instead, or just spin the popular picks.
  • Internet — used to load recipes and restaurant data.

Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.
